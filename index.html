How we choose our picks

We don’t subject shoes to our own timed lab tests or wear-test miles. Instead, we pore over published reviews from trusted cycling outlets, scour long-term owner forums for real-world failure points (like sole delamination or buckle breakage), and track resale value as a proxy for durability. Shoe fit is highly personal, so we prioritize models with consistent sizing, multiple width options, or generous return policies. Our final picks are the ones that earn consistent praise across years of ownership, not just first-ride impressions. We avoid novelty closures and exotic materials that haven’t proven themselves through seasons of sweat, rain, and hard shifts. A shoe that stays comfortable after 100 miles every day, and still looks good when you roll up to the café, earns our recommendation.
