A good pair of cycling shoes is one of the most impactful upgrades you can make. They transfer your power directly to the pedals, prevent hot spots, and keep you comfortable mile after mile. But with so many materials, closure systems, and sole stiffness ratings, choosing the right shoe can feel overwhelming. The key is to match the shoe to your riding style and body. Racers need a stiff, lightweight sole and a secure fit; endurance riders should prioritise all-day comfort and breathability; and anyone on a tight budget can still find excellent options that skip luxury details without sacrificing performance. Best for specific needs

Best for long days in the saddle

If you regularly ride 100km or more, comfort is king. The Shimano RC702 strikes the best balance: a stiff enough sole for efficient pedalling, but with enough flex and padding to avoid hot spots. The two Boa dials let you fine-tune pressure as your feet swell, and the wide sizing option accommodates feet that spread after hours of riding. It’s the shoe that disappears beneath you on a long century. Our pick: Shimano RC702 Road Cycling Shoe.

Best for all-out racing

When every watt counts and you’re gunning for a PR or a podium, the Specialized S-Works 7 is the gold standard. Its FACT Powerline sole is among the stiffest available, and the ultralight Dyneema upper keeps weight to a minimum. The fit is precise and secure, which is critical during hard accelerations. Just be prepared to pay a premium and accept a narrow fit. Our pick: Specialized S-Works 7 Road Shoe.

Best for narrow feet

Riders with narrow feet often struggle to find shoes that don’t feel sloppy. The Giro Empire ACC, with its lace closure and tapered last, cinches down evenly across the entire foot without leaving loose material. The kangaroo leather stretches slightly to match your foot shape, creating a custom-like fit. It’s a dream for distinct heel shapes and low-volume feet. Our pick: Giro Empire ACC Road Cycling Shoe.

Our verdict

No single shoe is perfect for everyone, so the trade-offs matter. Our overall winner, the Shimano RC702, gives you 90% of the performance of premium models for a fraction of the price. If your budget is tighter, the Shimano RP502 is a fantastic entry point. For pure racing speed, the Specialized S-Works 7 is unmatched, and for custom fit fans, the Sidi Wire 2‘s adjustability is hard to beat. And if you love classic looks and narrow feet, the Giro Empire ACC is a joy. Consider your priorities, try on if possible, and happy pedalling.
